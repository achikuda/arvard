5How this got built
The hardest design problem
wasn't the product. It was convincing
the org the problem was real.
When I brought Clarity forward, the first reaction from product was: "we already have PFM." I had to demonstrate that this wasn't an iteration on analytics — it was a different thesis about what a bank is for.
Move 1
Led with the business problem, not the design concept
Framed it as: customers don't trust their own balance, and that distrust erodes engagement and loyalty. Brought data before designs. Product directors need business problems, not feature ideas.
Business framing
Move 2
Ran research that made the problem undeniable
Customers carry an average of 8–11 recurring committed payments invisible to them at balance-check time. The gap between "balance" and "what's mine" wasn't a perception problem — it was a data problem we could solve.
Customer evidence
Move 3
Named it so product could own it
"Clarity" became a product concept, not a design concept. Product directors could put it in roadmap presentations without translating from design language. Names are organizational infrastructure.
Language as strategy
Move 4
Designed the roadmap to be easy to say yes to
Phased, de-risked, with a measurable gate at each stage. Leadership didn't have to bet on a vision — they could approve one quarter at a time.
Org alignment
Key lesson
"Beautiful ideas don't move organizations. Quantified pain does." Clarity became a roadmap commitment when paired with customer evidence and a clear business case.
6The plan
Two years. From a single number to a financial health platform.
Each phase proves something before asking for more. Year 1 ships Clarity and the first AI insight. Year 2 turns both into a platform that thinks alongside the customer.
Year 1
Foundation — 0 to 1
Ship Clarity. Prove engagement. Introduce the first smart moment.
Q1 – Q2
Clarity launches
Obligations engine detects recurring and committed payments from transaction history. Clarity number appears on the home screen — no user setup, no categories, no manual input. The right number is always visible.
Core feature
Q3
Goals layer ships
Customers set savings goals — Japan trip, school laptop, emergency fund. Clarity suggests allocation amounts when there's room. First touch of intelligence: the app starts working for the customer, not just recording history.
Goals & savings
Q4

Proactive nudge triggered by salary deposit. Single use case, tested, measured against savings activation and goal engagement. The moment the product stops being a ledger and becomes a guide.
AI insight
Year 2

Expand from a moment to a platform. Make it feel like the bank is thinking alongside you.
Q1 – Q2

Expand beyond salary deposits — spending dips, bill clusters, seasonal patterns all become triggers. Intelligence deepens without the customer noticing.
AI & ML
Q3

Surfaces goal ideas from behavioral patterns — "You spend ₾80/month on coffee. In 6 months, that's a flight to Istanbul." The product starts generating financial aspiration, not just tracking it.
Proactive intelligence

Replaces standard balance as the primary display on mobile, web, and ATM. The platform is complete — financial health is woven into every interaction, not a feature you have to find.

9What I learned
The biggest design decisions
weren't about the product.
01
Naming is organizational infrastructure.
"Clarity" wasn't chosen for marketing. It was chosen because product directors could put it in presentations without translating from design language. The right name makes an idea ownable across an organization — not just the team that created it.
02
A roadmap is a design artifact.
How you sequence a two-year plan — what you prove first, what you de-risk early — is a design decision as much as a product one. The order tells the organization what you believe. Getting the sequence right was as important as getting the features right.
03
Vision without evidence is just a deck.
Clarity became a roadmap commitment when I paired the vision with customer research and a business framing. The 8–11 hidden obligations number changed more conversations than any prototype. Quantified pain moves organizations.
Most banking software treats money as data. Clarity treats it as the thing that stands between a customer and the life they're trying to build.

The Clarity number is the first step — an honest figure, automatically calculated, always visible. The AI layer is what follows: a system that notices opportunities, surfaces them at the right moment, and makes it effortless to act.

This is what financial health looks like when it's built into the product — not a tab you have to find, not a feature you have to remember.
